## Design Context

### Users
Local Indiana tech community: developers looking for meetups and peers, newcomers exploring the Indy tech scene, and sponsors/employers who want to support events and reach this audience. They browse casually — probably on a laptop during a work break or on a phone between meetings. The site is a front door, not a daily tool.

### Brand Personality
**Warm, inviting, curious, driven.** Think: a well-lit coworking space where everyone knows the barista. Smart people doing real work, but zero pretension. The brand is the opposite of coastal tech bravado — it's midwestern sincerity with genuine ambition. These are local people who build tech companies in Indiana, and they're proud of that.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light. Airy, open, daytime energy. No dark mode aggression.
- **Palette anchor**: The signature yellow stays — it's been muted from its original brightness to a comfortable golden tone (`#eec042`). Broaden the palette with pale whites, warm off-whites, and cream tones that feel airy and modern. The yellow should be an accent and identity marker, not the entire canvas.
- **Typography direction**: Current Space Mono (headings) + Space Grotesk (body) pairing is in place. Keep it straightforward and readable — no ornate serifs, no decorative curly typefaces, nothing fussy.
- **Anti-references**: NOT edgy or confrontational like The Verge or X.com. No aggressive dark themes, no sharp editorial drama, no "we're disrupting everything" energy.
- **Tone**: Approachable and confident, never try-hard. The site should feel like an invitation, not a manifesto.

### Accessibility
- **Target**: WCAG AAA where feasible (7:1 contrast for normal text, 4.5:1 for large text). This is ambitious, especially with yellow — use the golden accent strategically against dark text rather than as a text color or background for body copy.
- **No known specific audience needs**, but honor reduced-motion preferences and maintain logical focus order.

### Design Principles
1. **Invitation over impression.** Every element should make someone feel welcome, not impressed. If a design choice feels like showing off, pull it back.
2. **Yellow is the handshake.** The golden accent is the brand's signature — use it to mark identity and draw the eye, not to fill space. A little yellow goes further than a lot.
3. **Honest warmth.** Midwestern sincerity — straightforward layouts, clear language, generous whitespace. No visual tricks, no dark patterns, no "growth hacking" aesthetics.
4. **Airy, not sterile.** The palette should breathe — pale backgrounds, open space, soft transitions. But never so minimal it feels cold or corporate. Warmth lives in the details: slightly warm whites, gentle shadows, rounded but not bubbly.
5. **Substance over style.** Real content (events, jobs, community proof) is the design. The visual layer should frame and support it, not compete with it.
